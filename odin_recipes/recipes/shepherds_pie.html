Shepherd’s Pie

Description

Ingredients

Serves: 3-4

Prep Time: ~2hrs

There's nothing more comforting than a homemade Shepherd’s Pie. This straightforward recipe will be easy for anyone to prepare, and satisfy anyone’s tastebuds.

  • Ground beef, 500 grams
  • Frozen peas, 1/2 a cup
  • Carrots, 3-4
  • Large white onion, 1
  • Celery, 2-3 stalks
  • Tomato paste, 100g
  • White sugar, about 1/4 cup
  • Worcester sauce, about a tablespoon
  • Salt and pepper
  • Potatoes, 4-5
  • Butter
  • Canola oil, about a Tablespoon
  • Garlic, 2 cloves
  • Parsley, about 4-5 sprigs

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 200℃
  2. First, let’s peel and cube the potatoes and bring them to a boil. Salt the water to taste.
  3. Next, let’s dice the onions and garlic and sautee them in a pot over low-medium heat.
  4. While those are softening up, chop the celery and carrots (after you peel them), and then add them to the sautee.
  5. Once the base veggies are softened, we’re ready to add the ground beef. Turn up he heat up a bit but be careful not to burn the garlic or onions.
  6. Add the parsley while the beef is browning. Make sure to turn drain a bit of the fat if you want a leaner meal.
  7. Once the beef is brown and all sides, we can add the tomato paste, sugar, and Worcester sauce and mix them in with the rest of the ingredients in the pot.
  8. Once they tomato paste has been incorporated with the beef and veggies add peas.
  9. At this point, the potatoes should be ready for mashing. Drain the water and transfer to a bowl.
  10. Add butter and mash until the potatoes are creamy.
  11. In an oven-safe dish, pour the beef sauce on the bottom and layer the mash potatoes on top.
  12. Often, people will run a fork across the top of the potato crust although, this is mainly decorative.
  13. Pop the dish in the oven and bake until the potato crust browns and the beef sauce bubbles up from the bottom. Shouldn’t longer than 30-40 mins.
